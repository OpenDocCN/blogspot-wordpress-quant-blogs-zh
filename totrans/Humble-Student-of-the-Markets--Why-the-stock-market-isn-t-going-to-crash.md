**Mid-week market update**

: I've had a number of questions from readers about the warnings of imminent market declines from SentimenTrader. In this

[post](https://www.sentimentrader.com//blog/this-led-to-declines-every-time-in-the-past-93-years/)

, Jason Goepfert's headline was "This Led to Declines Every Time in the Past 93 Years". He highlighted the market's poor breadth, as measured by the percentage of stocks above their 50 dma.

> Going back to the mid-1920's, there have only been a handful of dates with breaks like this. It happened in 1929, 1959, 1963, 1972, 1998, and 1999, and all of them ended up preceding losses in stocks.
