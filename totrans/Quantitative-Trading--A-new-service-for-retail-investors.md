# Quantitative Trading: A new service for retail investors

> 来源：[http://epchan.blogspot.com/2009/02/new-service-for-retail-investors.html#0001-01-01](http://epchan.blogspot.com/2009/02/new-service-for-retail-investors.html#0001-01-01)

Here is a new low-cost service called

[Alerts4All](http://www.alerts4all.com/)

that offers technical trading signals for retail investors. You can, for example, have an alert sent to you every time a "Double bottom" pattern occurs.

A much more advanced version of the service will be rolled out soon -- I saw a demo today where you can backtest your strategies online, combining different fundamental and/or technical variables as entry or exit signals. They also have some built-in models for you to adapt (e.g. a model based on

[The Little Book that Beats the Market](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0471733067?ie=UTF8&tag=quantitativet-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=0471733067)

by Joel Greenblatt.) More interestingly, you can look at other people's trading models and their historical and/or real-time performance.

Matlab or

[Alphacet](http://alphacet.com/)

it is not, but I think it will be quite useful for many retail traders. It might even be useful to professional traders who want a quick-and-dirty way to test out ideas.
