# Humble Student of the Markets: How much "runway" does China have left?

> 来源：[https://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2016/07/how-much-runway-does-china-have-left.html#0001-01-01](https://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2016/07/how-much-runway-does-china-have-left.html#0001-01-01)

As we await China's Q2 GDP report in the face of

[sharply lower June trade figures](http://www.reuters.com/article/us-china-economy-trade-idUSKCN0ZT0NO)

, it's useful to ponder once again the China tail-risk question. For years, analysts have been warning about impending doom in China because of the growing mountain of unsustainable debt (see

[Kyle Bass](http://www.cnbc.com/2016/02/10/kyle-bass-china-banks-may-lose-5-times-us-banks-subprime-losses-in-credit-crisis.html)

,

[Jim Chanos](http://www.businessinsider.com/jim-chanos-china-hard-pass-2016-5)

,

[Andy Xie](http://www.marketwatch.com/story/this-economist-thinks-china-is-headed-for-a-1929-style-depression-2016-06-30)

and 

[George Soros](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-04-20/soros-says-china-s-debt-fueled-economy-resembles-u-s-in-2007-08)

).

In the past, every time China has gotten to the edge of a hard landing, Beijing has confound the bears by kicking the can down the road and forestalling a crisis. That kind of "extend and pretend" strategy is perfectly viable if the road is long enough, but the key question for investors is, "How long is the road?"
