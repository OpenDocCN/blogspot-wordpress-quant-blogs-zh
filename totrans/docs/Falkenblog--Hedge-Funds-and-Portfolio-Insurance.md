# Falkenblog: Hedge Funds and Portfolio Insurance

> 来源：[http://falkenblog.blogspot.com/2008/10/hedge-funds-and-portfolio-insurance.html#0001-01-01](http://falkenblog.blogspot.com/2008/10/hedge-funds-and-portfolio-insurance.html#0001-01-01)

In the 1987 crash, there was an accelerator mechanism via portfolio insurance. Basically,

[Rubinstein, Leland and O'Brien](http://harvardbusinessonline.hbsp.harvard.edu/b01/en/common/item_detail.jhtml?id=294061)

got a bunch of investors to buy into portfolio insurance, which would sell securities as prices fell, emulating a put option position. In the crash, this just made things drastically worse.

But it seems hedge funds adopt this sort of strategy. Several prominent hedge funds

[moved to cash recently](http://online.wsj.com/article/SB122394318763531045.html)

: Steven Cohen, Israel Englander, John Paulson. That's a lot of money selling equities, buying yen, etc. No wonder such disparate popular hedge fund trades: long oil, the carry trade (long Aussie dollar, short yen), equities. They were all being unwound.
