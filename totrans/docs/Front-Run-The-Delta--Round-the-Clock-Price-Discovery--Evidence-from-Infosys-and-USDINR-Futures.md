# Front-Run The Delta: Round the Clock Price Discovery: Evidence from Infosys and USDINR Futures

> 来源：[https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/11/round-clock-price-discovery-evidence.html#0001-01-01](https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/11/round-clock-price-discovery-evidence.html#0001-01-01)

[Infosys](http://www.infosys.com/pages/index.aspx)

is dual listed in USD on the Nasdaq (

[INFY](http://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/infy)

) and in INR on the National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) (

[INFY](http://www.nseindia.com/live_market/dynaContent/live_watch/get_quote/GetQuote.jsp?symbol=INFY)

).  I do not have access to any spot

[INR](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_rupee)

facilities, however I do have available some NSE listed USD/INR

[futures](http://www.nseindia.com/products/content/derivatives/currency/cd.htm)

.  These futures allow for the currency-adjusted value of either equity to be derived.  Similar price transmission studies have been discussed using prices from

[Glencore](http://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/07/arbitraging-arbitrageur-case-of.html)

,

[RWE](http://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/07/how-knight-makes-market-evidence-from.html)

,

[Apple](http://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/08/apple-arbitrage-on-jobs-resignation.html)

, and

[Sino-Forest](http://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/08/quick-look-at-sino-forest.html)

, all four however using the spot FX as the pricing mechanism.

The two shares outright:

A European-listed, EUR-denominated equity would be advantageous for price transmission.  Further study could include the viability of using a EUR-denominated tech sector ETF as a pricing mechanism for the risk inherent between NSE and Nasdaq trading times.  

Research of this nature is, to date, inconclusive and will be continuous.
