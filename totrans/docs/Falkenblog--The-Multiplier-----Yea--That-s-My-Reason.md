Here in my state of Minnesota, we have a pretty large budget deficit, about 15% of spending, at $6.95 Billion for 2012\. That's unsustainable long term. The Democratic candidate, Margaret Andersen Kelliher, is running on a program of increasing r

[spending by $2B to create jobs](http://www.startribune.com/politics/98645054.html)

, but cutting $1B of '

[fraud and waste](http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2010/07/02/dfl-candidates-spar-in-debate/)

. In other words, spending $2B more. The idea is that it 'would put 50,000 Minnesotans to work constructing roads, bridges and buildings'.
