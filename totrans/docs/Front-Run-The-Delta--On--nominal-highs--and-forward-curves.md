# Front-Run The Delta: On "nominal highs" and forward curves

> 来源：[https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/07/on-all-time-highs-and-forward-curves.html#0001-01-01](https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2011/07/on-all-time-highs-and-forward-curves.html#0001-01-01)

CNBC is fond of citing "

[nominal highs](//www.google.com/search?q=cnbc:+%22nominal+high%22&ie=utf-8&oe=utf-8&aq=t&rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&client=firefox-a#sclient=psy&hl=en&client=firefox-a&hs=wuL&rls=org.mozilla:en-US%3Aofficial&source=hp&q=site:cnbc.com+%22nominal+high%22&aq=f&aqi=&aql=&oq=&pbx=1&bav=on.2,or.r_gc.r_pw.&fp=8164e4a79c46349b&biw=1280&bih=815)

" when silver, gold, crude, or any commodity for that matter, reach new levels.  What they fail to realize, perhaps out of ignorance (my guess), or because it's a watered-down poor excuse for a financial news network, is that the back of the curve is well bid above the

[front month](http://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/front-month-contract.asp)

prices always quoted in financial media.

If $1580/oz scares people,

[$1750](http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/07/13/us-markets-precious-idUSTRE7592IU20110713)

would send them into paralysis. 

I used the futures calendar

[abbreviations](http://www.ccstrade.com/references/month-codes/)

for the expirations on the chart of the curve.
