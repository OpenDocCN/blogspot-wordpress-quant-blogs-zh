# Falkenblog: The Genius in All of Us

> 来源：[http://falkenblog.blogspot.com/2010/10/genius-in-all-of-us.html#0001-01-01](http://falkenblog.blogspot.com/2010/10/genius-in-all-of-us.html#0001-01-01)

Now, a computer would have a very hard time with this, but as a native English speaker I read this with ease. As Marvin Minsky has pointed out (no relation to Hyman), computers still can't tell the difference between a dog and a cat, something my 3 year old daughter does with ease. The theme of

[Godel, Escher, Bach](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6del,_Escher,_Bach)

by Doug Hofstadter is that the mind is insanely good at finding patterns, and what is easy for us can be really difficult logically, and what is hard for us is easy for computers.

When I'm peevish, I'm amazed how how stupid most people are, how little original thought is created and appreciated, how faux rigor from false assumptions does not overcome them, how many

[phonies](http://www.bellmore-merrick.k12.ny.us/catcher2.html)

there are. But then, I remember stuff like this, and think that most us are quite clever.
