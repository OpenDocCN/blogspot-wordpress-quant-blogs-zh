# Front-Run The Delta: Fundamentals are in the back seat

> 来源：[https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2007/12/fundamentals-are-in-back-seat.html#0001-01-01](https://frontrunthedelta.blogspot.com/2007/12/fundamentals-are-in-back-seat.html#0001-01-01)

I have not updated this, or discussed investing, in several weeks. I've largely been sitting on the sidelines waiting for an emotional market to subside. In the mean time, I may be posting a few book reviews for

[Enhancing Trader Performance](http://www.amazon.com/Enhancing-Trader-Performance-Strategies-Psychology/dp/0470038667/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1198852180&sr=1-1)

and

[A Demon of Our Own Design](http://www.amazon.com/Demon-Our-Own-Design-Innovation/dp/0471227277/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1198852149&sr=8-1)

- both excellent reads thus far.

ETP is written by Dr. Brett Steenbarger, Ph.D. who runs the very popular

[TraderFeed](http://traderfeed.blogspot.com/)

blog - not about investment picks, but about investment psychology, arguably more valuable than any special recommendations.

Demon is written by Dr. Rick Bookstaber, Ph.D. who runs his own

[blog](http://rick.bookstaber.com/)

as well. I just found his this morning, so I haven't had a chance to peruse his thoughts, but judging from his book, I'm sure it contains a fair amount of

[wisdom](http://rick.bookstaber.com/2007/10/testimony-before-house-financial.html)

and

[insight](http://rick.bookstaber.com/2007/12/conversations-with-trading-desk.html)

.
