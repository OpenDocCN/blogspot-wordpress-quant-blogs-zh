# Physics Perspective: Why we do things... John Kay

> 来源：[http://physicsoffinance.blogspot.com/2014/02/why-we-do-things-john-kay.html#0001-01-01](http://physicsoffinance.blogspot.com/2014/02/why-we-do-things-john-kay.html#0001-01-01)

Not everything we do, not even most of it, is done for profit, or with other practical aims in mind.

[John Kay](http://www.johnkay.com/2014/02/19/philistines-may-carp-but-scientists-should-reach-for-the-sky)

:

> In 1969 Robert Wilson, director of the National Accelerator Laboratory, was testifying before the US Congress. He sought funding for a particle accelerator (forerunner of the Large Hadron Collider at Cern where the [Higgs boson](http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/7279a79a-c696-11e1-963a-00144feabdc0.html?siteedition=uk "Particle may prove part of a pantheon - FT.com") was discovered in 2012). Asked by Senator John Pastore how his project would help defeat the Russians, he responded: “It only has to do with the respect with which we regard one another . . . are we good painters, good sculptors, great poets . . . new knowledge has nothing to do directly with defending our country except to help make it worth defending.”
