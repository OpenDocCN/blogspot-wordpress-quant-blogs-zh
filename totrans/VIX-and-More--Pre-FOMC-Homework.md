# VIX and More: Pre-FOMC Homework

> 来源：[http://vixandmore.blogspot.com/2007/09/pre-fomc-homework.html#0001-01-01](http://vixandmore.blogspot.com/2007/09/pre-fomc-homework.html#0001-01-01)

OK, so it’s more like recommended reading than homework, but the links below have given me a fair amount to contemplate in the past few days and they may be a good way to mark time between now and Tuesday at 2:15 p.m. EDT.

*   Vinny Catalano puts the current situation succinctly:  “[It’s a Bull Market ‘til it Ain’t](http://vinnycatalano.blogspot.com/2007/09/technical-thursdays-its-bull-market-til.html)”
*   Ben Bittrolff at [The Financial Ninja](http://benbittrolff.blogspot.com/) weaves together a more detailed tapestry, with some charts, news and commentary that offer a [good snapshot of the pre-FOMC markets](http://benbittrolff.blogspot.com/2007/09/kill-greenspan-put.html)
*   James Picerno at [The Capital Spectator](http://www.capitalspectator.com/) on the [Fed’s balancing act](http://www.capitalspectator.com/archives/2007/09/bernankes_big_t.html) vis-a-vis interest rates and the strength of the dollar
*   [Accrued Interest](http://accruedint.blogspot.com/) (yes, that really is a bond-only blog) [makes the case](http://accruedint.blogspot.com/2007/09/it-is-pointless-to-resist.html) for the Fed cutting to 3.75%
*   Mike Shedlock of [Mish’s Global Economic Trend Analysis](http://globaleconomicanalysis.blogspot.com/) on why the credit ponzi scheme is a [disaster that cannot be averted](http://globaleconomicanalysis.blogspot.com/2007/09/crisis-in-asset-backed-commercial-paper.html)
*   …and why [swaptions are a large part of the problem](http://globaleconomicanalysis.blogspot.com/2007/09/swaptions-and-turmoil-in-financials.html)
*   Eric Roseman at [Roseman Eruptions](http://rosemanblog.sovereignsociety.com/) says <place st="on">Asia</place> is dodging the bullet because of a [lack of leverage in private equity deals](http://rosemanblog.sovereignsociety.com/2007/09/1990s-lessons-l.html)
*   Tim Knight of [The Slope of Hope](http://www.slopeofhope.com/) looks at [hope vs. panic zones](http://www.slopeofhope.com/2007/09/color-zones.html) for the RUT, suggesting we should follow the next big move instead of fading it
*   [Headline Charts](http://headlinecharts.blog.com/) looks at 17 years of advance decline data and says we [need a confirming A-D line](http://headlinecharts.blog.com/2075952/) to move higher
*   Menzie Chinn at [Econbrowser](http://www.econbrowser.com/) explains why the [labor market is worse than you think](http://www.econbrowser.com/archives/2007/09/just_how_bad_is.html)
*   Monty Silverman of [Econocator](http://www.econocator.com/) recaps [what the banks have to say about weak payroll data](http://www.econocator.com/2007/09/10/us-economic-indicators/payroll-reactions/)
*   Paul Kedrosky at [Infectious Greed](http://paul.kedrosky.com/) points out the distinction Jeff Saut makes between 1988-1992 and the current housing crisis.  What’s different now?  [Rising inventories](http://paul.kedrosky.com/archives/2007/09/11/housing_cycles.html).
*   …and Barry Ritholtz of [The Big Picture](http://bigpicture.typepad.com/) [draws some parallels to the anchoring effect](http://bigpicture.typepad.com/comments/2007/09/real-estate-inv.html) mindset of investors whose bad trades turn into ‘long-term investments’
*   Monty Silverman of [Econocator](http://www.econocator.com/) notes that according to Merrill Lynch, [assets have not (yet) priced in a recession](http://www.econocator.com/2007/09/12/insights/have-asset-markets-priced-in-a-recession/)
*   Chad Brand at [The Period Capitalist](http://www.peridotcapitalist.com/) with some reminders on how to best think about [insider buying vs. insider selling](http://www.peridotcapitalist.com/2007/09/insider-selling-could-mean-anything.html)
*   [John Brasher’s Covered Call Blog](http://www.callwriter.com/blog/) discusses [covered call plays on the i-banks](http://www.callwriter.com/blog/2007/09/10/brokers-report-earnings-this-week/) going into earnings (Lehman leads off the earnings parade [Tuesday BMO](http://www.earnings.com/earning.asp?date=20070918&sortby=startdate&client=cb))
*   Adam Warner at [Daily Options Report](http://adamsoptions.blogspot.com/) on [what you can learn from options volume](http://adamsoptions.blogspot.com/2007/09/my-take-on-reading-options-volumes.html)
*   [Ticker Sense](http://tickersense.typepad.com/) drills down on [deteriorating earnings in the financial sector](http://tickersense.typepad.com/ticker_sense/2007/09/financial-secto.html)
*   [The CXO Advisory Group](http://www.cxoadvisory.com/) looks at a strategy to [sell puts for 40% per month](http://www.cxoadvisory.com/blog/external/blog9-10-07/) (*what* color is that swan?)
*   Barry Ritholtz again, this time with a [new historical twist](http://bigpicture.typepad.com/comments/2007/09/are-the-rules-d.html) on 9-to-1 days
*   David Merkel of [The Aleph Blog](http://alephblog.com/) has an [instructive look back](http://alephblog.com/2007/09/11/my-911-experience/) at fear vs. rationality in a post-9/11 world
*   Mebane Faber at [World Beta](http://worldbeta.blogspot.com/) highlights some [recent academic papers](http://worldbeta.blogspot.com/2007/09/alphaletters-selected-journal-paper.html) of note
*   Barry Ritholtz (can you say [hat trick](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hat_trick)?) has a copy of the extremely cool Information Architects’ [map of the web](http://bigpicture.typepad.com/comments/2007/09/web-trend-map-2.html) (someday this will be great archival history)
*   My chimp is smarter than your baby?  Maybe not, but [it’s very close](http://infoproc.blogspot.com/2007/09/baby-vs-chimp.html), notes Steve Hsu at [Information Processing](http://infoproc.blogspot.com/)
*   Speaking of babies, no parent should be without a [$170 pocket camcorder](http://www.engadget.com/2007/09/13/aiptek-cranks-out-720p-pocket-camcorder-for-170/), because you never know what’s coming next…
