# Humble Student of the Markets: Pitchfork watch

> 来源：[https://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2009/06/pitchfork-watch.html#0001-01-01](https://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2009/06/pitchfork-watch.html#0001-01-01)

I

[previously noted](http://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2009/04/third-way-in-inflationdeflation-debate.html)

that Simon Johnson, the former chief economist at the IMF, wrote that “unless the government of the day is willing to take on the entrenched elites [read Wall Street and hedgies] and force them to take the pain, the path of least resistance is rising inflation and possibly hyperinflation.”

**Social backlash?**

Back on

[May 3](http://humblestudentofthemarkets.blogspot.com/2009/05/inflation-watch-linkfest.html)

I wrote that the

[New York Magazine](http://nymag.com/news/businessfinance/56151/)

reports a quote from a Citibank executive as an illustration of the culture of entitlement:

> No offense to Middle America, but if someone went to Columbia or Wharton, [even if] their company is a fumbling, mismanaged bank, why should they all of a sudden be paid the same as the guy down the block who delivers restaurant supplies for Sysco out of a huge, shiny truck?

When I start seeing items like

[this](http://www.doonesbury.com/strip/dailydose/index.html?up_year_month=200905&up_day=31)

in the popular press, there is little question that the social backlash is starting.
